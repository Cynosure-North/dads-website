About Dave I moved to Nelson in 2002 to bring up my 2 children in a vibrant, positive community.  Originally from London, I came to New Zealand in 1990 - initially for a year, but haven't left yet!  I met my wife, Ali (a kiwi), in Wellington.  Whilst I enjoyed the energy that Wellington had to offer, Nelson was a natural fit for me.  I enjoy the outdoors, small communities and easy access to recreational activities.  I am now at the stage of life I am keen to use my experience and energy to give back, to ensure that the positive energy in Nelson will remain for future generations. I studied marine biology in Scotland, before working in the Information Technology industry for 15 years.  Whilst in Wellington, I saw an advert for a trial flight, and, having always been interested in aviation, I thought I would give it a go.  30 years later, after a career as an airline pilot I am about ready to come back to earth and move on to the next stage of my life.  Initially working for a local airline, I spent 15 years at Air Nelson (bnow part of Air New Zealand) as a captain on domestic services.  I then worked for several years for the air ambulance in Wellington, before again joining our local airline as a part time captain.  Until recently I was also the Chief Flying Instrucotr at the Nelson Aero Club. My other passion os music.  For many years I was part of Nelson HJazz club - as president, involved in runnig the Jazz Festival, and also as a player in the big band.  I currently play in the Nelson Symphony Orchestra and am the chair of the committee.  I also play regualrly in small groups in the communtioy and retirement homes. As a scientist I understand the long-term context and science of climate change and the need to transform our systems of transport, energy and food production. The next Council term will be a time of great change, and more than ever we need Councillors who understand the complexities of the path ahead, communicate well and engage with the community, and are prepared to lead and to make positive change.  We are now aware f the impact we have on the planet, and we can no longer just ignore it.  We won't pay the price - our children and their children will bear the cost of our inaction so we need tomake changes that lessen our impact without impacting our quality of life. I am a great believer in quality of life over standard of living.  Standard of living is measured in material wealth.  I get more please from quality of life.  Safe, vibrant communtiies.  Pollution free environments, safe beaches tand rivers to swim in, mountains to ride in and explore, national parks to enjoy.  These are thereasures that New Zealend, and particulalry Whakatu Nelson have to offer.  Willingness to embrace new cultures, new experiences, tolerance are the secret to enjoying life to the full.  Skills and Experience Used to working in community organisations to collaboratively achieve goals.
